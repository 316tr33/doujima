Tokaido 53 Stations

Explore 53 post towns from Edo to Kyoto

Yamashiro Province The endpoint of Tokaido, Gateway to Kyoto

Sanjo Ohashi

Sanjo Ohashi

The endpoint of the Tokaido Road at Kyoto's Sanjo Bridge. Built in 1590 under Toyotomi Hideyoshi's command, it served as the western terminus of the Tokaido during the Edo period, where countless travelers passed through. The historic bridge still spans the Kamo River today.

Oumi Province Post towns along Lake Biwa, Junction of Tokaido and Nakasendo

Otsu

Otsu

The final station on the Tokaido. Located on the shores of Lake Biwa, it is a cultural post town famous for Otsu-e paintings.

Kusatsu

Kusatsu

A transportation hub where the Tokaido and Nakasendo meet. The preserved honjin (inn) conveys the atmosphere of those times.

Ishibe

Ishibe

A post town said to be the origin of the phrase "Ishibe Kinkichi," describing a stubborn, inflexible person.

Minakuchi

Minakuchi

A post town that prospered as a castle town of Minakuchi Castle. It has a prestigious history as a lodging place for Tokugawa Shoguns during their trips to Kyoto.

Tsuchiyama

Tsuchiyama

The first post town entering Oumi Province after crossing Suzuka Pass. It is also a tea-producing region famous for Tsuchiyama tea.

Ise Province Historic post towns facing Ise Bay

Sakanoshita

Sakanoshita

A mountain post town at the foot of Suzuka Pass. It prospered as a base for crossing the pass and features a waterfall depicted in Katsushika Hokusai's ukiyo-e.

Seki

Seki

A post town with the most beautifully preserved Edo-period streetscape. Designated as an Important Preservation District for Groups of Traditional Buildings.

Kameyama

Kameyama

A post town that prospered as a castle town of Kameyama Castle. Utagawa Hiroshige depicted a daimyo procession ascending the slope leading to the castle gate.

Shono

Shono

A small post town in a quiet rural area. A place where traditional Japanese countryside scenery remains and time flows leisurely.

Ishiyakushi

Ishiyakushi

Only about 2.7km from Shono-juku. The second shortest distance between stations on the Tokaido, with Ishiyakushi-ji Temple from which the name derives.

Yokkaichi

Yokkaichi

A post town that developed as a commercial port. Still occupies an important position as an industrial city in Mie Prefecture.

Kuwana

Kuwana

A port town on Ise Bay famous for Shichiri no Watashi ferry crossing. Honda Tadakatsu, one of the Four Heavenly Kings of Tokugawa, became the first domain lord and developed it as a castle town of Kuwana Castle.

Owari Province Atsuta Shrine's temple town, Center of Nagoya

Miya

Miya

A post town that developed as a temple town of Atsuta Shrine. One of Japan's three major shrines, famous for the Kusanagi sword.

Narumi

Narumi

A traditional crafts post town famous for Narumi shibori tie-dyeing. Beautiful indigo dyeing techniques are still passed down today.

Mikawa Province Birthplace of Tokugawa Ieyasu, Home of Mikawa warriors

Chiryu

Chiryu

Present-day Chiryu City. A post town famous for Chiryu Shrine and horse markets where many merchants gathered.

Okazaki

Okazaki

A castle town famous as the birthplace of Tokugawa Ieyasu. A historic tourist destination centered around Okazaki Castle.

Fujikawa

Fujikawa

A post town famous for purple wheat. The beautiful Bohana (entrance marker) depicted in Hiroshige's ukiyo-e has been reconstructed.

Akasaka

Akasaka

A small post town adjacent to Goyu-juku. A place where historic pine tree-lined avenues are beautifully preserved.

Goyu

Goyu

A post town famous for beautiful pine-lined avenues. Prospered as the political and cultural center of Mikawa Province.

Yoshida

Yoshida

The center of eastern Mikawa that prospered as a castle town of Yoshida Castle. Now developed as modern Toyohashi City.

Futagawa

Futagawa

A precious post town where the honjin building remains. Strongly retains the atmosphere of an Edo-period post town.

Totomi Province Post towns along Lake Hamana, Tenryu River, and Enshu coast

Shirasuka

Shirasuka

A post town on a plateau facing the Enshu coast. From Shiomizaka Park within the post town, a magnificent view of the Pacific Ocean spreads out.

Arai

Arai

A post town with an important checkpoint rivaling Hakone. A historically significant point known for strict inspections.

Maisaka

Maisaka

A post town that prospered as a ferry crossing on Lake Hamana. Still offers beautiful lake views and fresh seafood, with eel dishes being particularly famous.

Hamamatsu

Hamamatsu

A castle town famous for eel dishes. The central city of western Shizuoka Prefecture that also prospers with the musical instrument industry.

Mitsuke

Mitsuke

Said to be named "Mitsuke" (to see/find) because travelers heading east from Kyoto first saw Mt. Fuji from here.

Fukuroi

Fukuroi

Famous as the "middle point" of the Tokaido 53 Stations. A modern area that prospers with the tea industry and serves as the starting point for Enshu Sanzan pilgrimage.

Kakegawa

Kakegawa

A post town that prospered as a castle town of Kakegawa Castle. The beautifully reconstructed castle tower is still a highlight today.

Nissaka

Nissaka

A mountain post town famous for the night-crying stone legend. A historic post town at the foot of Sayo-no-Nakayama Pass.

Kanaya

Kanaya

A post town with the Oigawa Railway operating steam locomotives. In the Edo period, it was famous for the Oi River crossing.

Suruga Province Scenic areas at the foot of Mt. Fuji along Suruga Bay

Shimada

Shimada

A post town that prospered with the Oi River crossing. The stage of "While Hakone's eight ri can be crossed on horseback, the Oi River cannot be crossed at all."

Fujieda

Fujieda

Prospered as a castle town of Tanaka Castle that Tokugawa Ieyasu often visited, and as a junction point of the Tanuma Highway leading to Sagara, a salt production area.

Okabe

Okabe

A mountain post town known as a Shizuoka tea production area. The Edo-period inn "Kashiwaya" still remains.

Mariko

Mariko

A post town famous for tororo (grated yam) soup. A tororo soup shop operating in the same location for 400 years still attracts many tourists seeking the specialty.

Fuchu

Fuchu

Developed as a castle town of Sunpu Castle. Also a political center where Tokugawa Ieyasu spent his later years.

Ejiri

Ejiri

A port town functioning as present-day Shimizu Port. The birthplace of Jirocho of Shimizu, said to be the greatest boss along the highway, remains.

Okitsu

Okitsu

A post town that prospered as a villa area for politicians during the Meiji era. A place that became a stage for history and politics.

Yui

Yui

A post town famous for sakura shrimp. Utagawa Hiroshige depicted the spectacular view of Mt. Fuji and Suruga Bay from Satta Pass.

Kambara

Kambara

A post town famous for Utagawa Hiroshige's snow scene. The charm lies in namako-walled residences and Western-style buildings from the Taisho era creating a retro townscape.

Yoshiwara

Yoshiwara

A post town at the foot of Mt. Fuji. For travelers from the east, "Hidari Fuji" (Mt. Fuji on the left) was famous, where the road curves sharply making Mt. Fuji visible on the left.

Hara

Hara

A famous spot depicted in Utagawa Hiroshige's ukiyo-e as a post town with spectacular views of Mt. Fuji. Beautiful scenery remains today.

Numazu

Numazu

A port town facing Suruga Bay proud of fresh seafood. The pine-lined avenue of Senbon Matsubara, famous as the best scenic spot on the Tokaido, continues along the coast.

Izu Province Mt. Fuji's spring water, Gateway to Hakone

Mishima Station

Mishima

A post town famous for Mt. Fuji's spring water. A place that prospered from ancient times as a temple town of Mishima Taisha Shrine.

Sagami Province Hakone checkpoint and scenic post towns along Sagami Bay

Hakone Station

Hakone

A mountain checkpoint post town feared as the Tokaido's greatest challenge. An important rest stop for travelers crossing the steep mountain path of Hakone Hachiri, it continues to be loved worldwide as one of Japan's leading hot spring tourist destinations.

Odawara Station

Odawara

The last major post town in the Kanto region that prospered as a castle town of the Hojo clan during the Warring States period. A historic tourist destination centered on Odawara Castle, also known for specialty products like kamaboko and umeboshi. Travelers gathered strength here before the steep mountain path of Hakone.

Oiso Station

Oiso

A post town with beautiful pine-lined avenues overlooking Sagami Bay. Loved as a villa area for politicians and literati during the Meiji era, now open to the public as Meiji Memorial Oiso Estate.

Hiratsuka Station

Hiratsuka

An important post town as a ferry landing on the Sagami River. Located in the center of the Sagami Plain, now famous for the Tanabata Festival. Prospered as a key point on the Tokaido during the Edo period.

Fujisawa Station

Fujisawa

A post town that developed as a temple town of Yugyoji Temple. Also served as a junction point for the Enoshima Road, the pilgrimage route to Enoshima Shrine.

Totsuka Station

Totsuka

As the first overnight stop for travelers departing from Edo, and as a junction with the Kamakura Road, it bustled with many travelers.

Musashi Province Gateway to Edo, Starting point of Tokaido

Hodogaya Station

Hodogaya

A post town facing the difficult passage of Gondazaka slope. A monument of the bridge depicted in Utagawa Hiroshige's ukiyo-e has been installed at the original location.

Kanagawa Station

Kanagawa

A historically important post town as an opening port at the end of the Edo period. Temples that served as consulates for various countries at the time of opening still exist today.

Kawasaki Station

Kawasaki

A post town famous for the Rokugo ferry crossing on the Tama River. The specialty "Nara-chameshi" (Nara tea rice) appears in "Tokaidochu Hizakurige" (Shank's Mare).

Shinagawa Station

Shinagawa

The first post town on the Tokaido. During the Edo period, pleasure quarters and teahouses lined the streets, bustling with many travelers.

Nihonbashi

Nihonbashi

The starting point of the Tokaido 53 Stations and the beginning of all five major highways. From the Edo period to the present, it has prospered as the center of Japanese transportation and commerce, and many travelers began their long journey from here.
