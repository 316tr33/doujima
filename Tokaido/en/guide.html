Equipment to Bring

● Essential Equipment

  • 👟 Comfortable walking shoes
  • 🎒 Lightweight backpack
  • 💧 Water bottle/beverage (1L or more)
  • 📱 Smartphone and mobile battery
  • 🩹 First aid supplies (band-aids, disinfectant)

● Recommended Items

  • 👒 Hat and sunglasses
  • ☀️ Sunscreen
  • 🤧 Towel and handkerchief
  • ☔ Lightweight rain gear
  • 🍪 Snacks and energy food

● Additional Seasonal Equipment

Summer: Cooling towel, salt supplements, insect repellent, quick-dry underwear

Winter: Warm clothing, windproof wear, heat packs

■

What You Need to Know

● Basic Walking Precautions

  • ⚠️ Be careful of traffic along roads
  • 💧 Stay hydrated to prevent dehydration
  • 👥 Stay with the group and follow the guide's instructions
  • 🏃 Avoid overexertion and walk at your own pace

● Health Management Tips

  • ☀️ On hot days, wear a hat and sunscreen to prevent heatstroke
  • ❄️ On cold days, dress warmly and protect against wind
  • 👟 Wear well-broken-in shoes to prevent blisters
  • 😴 Get plenty of sleep the night before

● Contact the Guide Immediately in Emergencies

If you feel unwell or get injured, don't push yourself—contact the tour guide immediately. The guide will provide appropriate assistance.

■

Tips for Enjoyment

● Photography Tips

  • 📷 Photograph post town markers and ichirizuka milestones for memories
  • 🗻️ Capture scenic spots with views of Mount Fuji, like Satta Pass
  • 🏯 Capture the atmosphere of historic buildings and old townscapes
  • 👥 Don't forget commemorative photos with fellow participants

● Enjoy Local Interactions

  • 🍜 Taste local specialties at each post town (sakura shrimp, Hamamatsu gyoza, etc.)
  • 🛍️ Check out local products at souvenir shops
  • ☕ Take a break at local cafes and enjoy the atmosphere
  • 💬 Chat with locals to discover hidden spots

● Memory-Making Tips

  • 📝 Keep a walking journal of your impressions each day
  • 🌌 Collect post town stamps and pamphlets
  • 🌅 Enjoy beautiful early morning scenery and sunsets
  • 👂 Listen to the guide's historical stories to deepen your understanding of the Tokaido
